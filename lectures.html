Courses & Lectures

Educational offerings in soft bioelectronics and related fields

Educational Programs

Prof. Jaehong Lee teaches courses spanning graduate and undergraduate levels at DGIST, covering soft materials and devices, semiconductor physics, and micro/nano engineering. Our courses combine theoretical foundations with practical knowledge to prepare students for careers in advanced materials and device research.

RT 659

Soft Materials and Devices

Graduate-level course covering the fundamentals and applications of soft materials in electronic devices. Topics include polymer-based electronics, flexible and stretchable device design, soft sensors, actuators, and biocompatible materials for wearable and implantable systems.

3 Credits

Fall Semester

Graduate Level

EE 304

Introduction to Semiconductor Physics

Undergraduate course covering the fundamental physics of semiconductor materials and devices. Topics include crystal structure, energy band theory, carrier transport, p-n junctions, MOSFETs, and optoelectronic devices, providing a solid foundation for advanced electronics studies.

3 Credits

Spring Semester

Undergraduate Level

MECH 402

Micro/Nano Engineering

Undergraduate course on the principles and applications of micro- and nano-scale engineering. Topics include microfabrication techniques, MEMS/NEMS devices, lithography, thin film processing, etching, and their applications in sensors, actuators, and biomedical devices.

3 Credits

Spring Semester

Undergraduate Level

Weekly Paper Seminar

Weekly Paper Seminar

The lab organizes a weekly paper seminar featuring discussions of recent papers and advances in soft bioelectronics, flexible electronics, wearable sensors, soft robotics, and related fields. We analyze cutting-edge research from the literature and discuss implications for our ongoing work.
