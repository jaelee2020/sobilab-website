Join Our Lab

We're recruiting talented researchers to advance soft bioelectronics

Why Join SoBiLab?

The Soft Bioelectronics Lab is an innovative research group at DGIST working at the intersection of materials science, electronics, and biomedical engineering. We create cutting-edge wearable and implantable devices that can improve human health and enable new forms of human-robot interaction.

Our team is collaborative, interdisciplinary, and committed to advancing both fundamental science and practical applications. We offer an environment where you can develop as a researcher, contribute to impactful projects, and build your academic career.

Graduate Student Opportunities

Master's Degree (M.S.)

Two-year program focusing on fundamental research skills, coursework, and thesis research. M.S. students work on focused projects that contribute to ongoing research areas.

Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree in engineering, physics, materials science, or related field
  • Strong foundation in mathematics and basic sciences
  • Research experience is a plus but not required
  • English language proficiency

Ph.D. Degree (Ph.D.)

Four to five-year program for students committed to deep research and innovation. Ph.D. students lead their own research projects, publish papers, and become independent researchers.

Qualifications:

  • Master's degree or strong Bachelor's degree record
  • Research experience and publication record preferred
  • Strong motivation for independent research
  • English language proficiency

Postdoctoral Opportunities

Postdoctoral Research Fellow

We welcome experienced postdoctoral researchers interested in independent research projects. Postdocs at SoBiLab can develop their own research direction within our lab's areas of focus, collaborate with graduate students, and prepare for faculty positions.

Position Details:

  • 2-3 year initial appointment
  • Competitive salary and benefits
  • Research funding support
  • Mentorship for career development

Requirements:

  • Ph.D. in relevant field
  • Proven research track record
  • Strong publication record
  • English language proficiency

Current Research Areas

Flexible & Stretchable Electronics

Bio-inspired pressure sensors, paper-based interactive devices, stretchable conductor networks. We are seeking students with interests in materials engineering, polymer chemistry, and device physics.

Wearable Sensors & Smart Textiles

Textile-integrated electronic sensors, smart gloves for human-machine interaction, real-time physiological monitoring. Positions available for students with background in materials, electronics, or biomedical engineering.

Implantable Bioelectronics

Biodegradable electronics, electronic sutures, biocompatible materials. Students should have interest in biomedical applications, materials science, or pharmaceutical engineering.

Soft Robotics & Tactile Sensing

Proprioceptive sensing systems, interactive soft actuators, human-robot collaboration. Ideal for roboticists, mechanical engineers, and control systems specialists.
